FAQ

Questions, answered.

I'm still doing my undergraduate. Can I still apply?

Yes. Undergraduates are welcome, provided you have a strong foundation in deep learning, NLP, and LLMs and can commit to the program's schedule.

When does the program run?

Applications open July 1, 2026, with two deadlines — Early Bird (July 25) and Final (August 10). The 12-week program runs from early September to December 2026, fully online. Top teams present at the GStar Summit in 2027.

What are the graduation requirements?

Completion of the core curriculum, both graded assignments, and a mentored capstone — presented to the panel of lecturers, guest lecturers, and mentors.

What's the enrollment process?

Submit your application before your chosen deadline. Selected candidates complete an entrance test and a final evaluation, after which admission and scholarship decisions are made.

How much does it cost, and is aid available?

Tuition is $4,500 for the full program. Scholarships are available upon request, up to 100% coverage, based on merit and need.

How and when are decisions sent?

Decisions are sent directly to your registered email on a rolling basis after each evaluation round.

What language is the program in?

English — all instruction, materials, and presentations, reflecting the program's global standard.

How much time should I plan each week?

Plan for approximately 15–20 hours per week across instruction, assignments, reading, office hours, and capstone work. Actual time varies by background and project scope.

Are sessions live, and what timezone is used?

The program is online and includes scheduled cohort activities. The final live-session schedule, timezone, attendance policy, and recording availability are confirmed before enrollment.

Do I need my own GPU or cloud budget?

Some assignments require accelerated compute. Exact hardware requirements, available credits, and any participant-paid compute costs are communicated before enrollment.

Who owns the capstone work?

Project publication, code licensing, confidentiality, and intellectual-property terms are defined in the enrollment and capstone agreements. Review them before committing a proprietary idea.
